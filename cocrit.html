Co-Crit

10,000 businesses have permanently shut down during the pandemic - Fortune

To help fight COVID-19, I wanted to drive traffic to struggling businesses and help individuals make more informed decisions. So, I created Co-Crit, a mobile app that crowdsources information regarding business' coronavirus safety measures.

Co-Crit is currently pending approval on the Google Play and Apple App Stores

Integrated with all businesses via the Google Places API
Upload reviews to our database provided by Firebase
View business' statistics before visiting
See how safe others felt at a business
Research

Currently, most COVID-19 apps are sponsored by the government and are centered on contact tracing. There is no way for individuals to know how safe a given business is before visiting. Numerous interviewees have expressed frustration when visiting a business that is not enforcing standard safety proceedures such as social distancing.

Design Considerations
  • Determining what factors should be released in a minimum viable product
  • Implemented a scaling algorithm to account for different devices' width to height ratio
  • Used familiar, simple icons and layout to increase usability and learnability
  • Considered layout adjustment for phones with back buttons on the bezzle and notches for the camera
  • Considered device accessibility features such as orientation and layout/font size options
Insights and Takeaways
  • My first mobile app showed that making an app is hard work and has countless factors to consider!
  • After switching from testing on an emulator to a real device, I found that the app's usability changes completely.
  • I will be more intentional in planning how an interface will scale on differently sized devices from the start.
  • I learned how to use React Native, Firebase, and various Google APIs.
