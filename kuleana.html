Kuleana Website Prototype

Kuleana is a startup that specializes in plant based raw tuna. There are several companies that make vegetable based meat substitutes, such as Beyond Meat, but currently, there is yet to be an established seafood equivalent. Kuleana hopes to capitalize on this by creating 100 percent plant based, sushi-grade raw tuna so that consumers can enjoy fish without the environmental and ethical consequences.

For this school project, my team and I had two weeks to design an interface for Kuleana. We drew sketches; created wireframes; designed an interactive, high-fidelity prototype; and, conducting user testing on our final prototype.

Hi-Fi Prototype

We used Figma to create a hi-fi prototype and made design choices based on hierarchy, interaction, and navigation.

Hierarchy: We modified fonts and font sizes based on the text's function. For example, we had one main title font and font size. Smaller titles also followed a consistent styling and size, and we also had a consistent paragraph font size.

Interaction: We focused on making interactions simple. For example, we provided explicit instructions on where to hover on our Mission page to eliminate any possible confusion. Furthermore, clickable elements were enlarged and have a green border upon hover to make it clear that they can be clicked.

Navigation: Our navbar contained the main pages of the site, which were accessible from every page. Certain pages also featured buttons that would navigate to different parts of the site such as the checkout button on the shop page.

Studio Crit

We also had a class critique where we our peers and a guest industry critic evaluated our prototype. The following table lists the feedback we received and the changes we made in response.

Feedback Changes
The purchase button on the Recipe page is confusing because it is unclear whether the user is purchasing only the tuna or all components required by the recipe. We replaced the purchase button with a download button, which we believe would be more helpful for users since we also added a Shop page to the nav bar.
Add a feature to edit the amount of tuna someone has purchased. We added an edit button next to the amount of tuna on the checkout page so users can change their order.
The prototype has an inconsistent use of hover. Sometimes images expand without having a green border and are not clickable. We removed the unclickable, expanding images on the Home page to focus on simplicity and usability.
Consider two different use cases: exploring vs. purchasing. Instead of having a purchase section on the home page, which may be a little aggressive, we added a shop page on the navbar.
Purchase should be more visible. It is difficult to remember that the option to purchase is on the Home page since it is at the bottom. Added a shop page, which is dedicated to purchasing and now contains the information in the purchase section that was originally on the home page.
The white border makes the website look like it is intended for mobile. We expanded the size of the screen to eliminate the white space on the sides for 15 inch laptops.
Font size is too big (also makes it look like it is intended for mobile). We reduced the overall font size throughout the site.
User Testing

With our updated prototype, we conducted our own usability tests via UserTesting.com. Because the purpose of our website is to give an explanation of what Kuleana is and to convert browsing users into customers, we created subtasks to test whether the prototype is clear, intuitive, and effective in driving purchases.

Subtasks

  1. In your own words, please describe what Kuleana is and what we are selling.
  2. Please explain the purpose of the Mission and Community pages. Explain how these pages contributed to your opinion of Kuleana and discuss whether they were effective in accomplishing the purpose that you stated.
  3. Learn one way to use Kuleana's tuna to make a delicious meal by downloading a recipe! Please describe whether this feature would drive you to make a purchase and why/why not.
  4. Purchase a pound or more of tuna and describe whether this process was intuitive or not and why.

We also asked users the following:

  1. Was the website designed in a way that made it easy for anyone to understand Kuleana and what the company stands for?
  2. Did the information provided on the website drive you to make a purchase? Please discuss what information would be more effective in doing so.
  3. What did you dislike about this site's usability and intuitiveness? How would you improve it?

Analysis of Results
  • Though Subtask 1 was intended to be given after users explored the website, the Home page was sufficient.
  • In Subtask 2, all users were able to clearly relay what Kuleana’s mission and product are.
  • User 1 and 2 wished they knew what the tuna is made from. In contrast, User 3 said that the company mission, recipes, and community images made his mouth water and made him eager to purchase our product.
  • All users accomplished Tasks 3 and 4 less than two minutes and noted that everything was straightforward. Users noted that downloading a recipe would drive purchases because the described meals are much more appealing than just the tuna.
  • User 3 explained how the edit button would take him back to the previous page and then allow him to select a different order before he even clicked it.
  • Users accomplished the primary task in approximately seven minutes.
Errors and User Suggestions
  • Two users did not realize that the Mission page held more information at the bottom of the page in one of the bubbles. User 1 also did not realize that the page was scrollable.
  • User 1 never clicked on the clickable post on the Community page.
  • To improve the design, all users suggested making pages more animated and interesting.
  • User 3 suggested including a title for the product on the Shop page for scalability if we expand our product line.
Insights and Takeaways
  • Though certain pages can be enhanced with more animations, our tests revealed that the prototype’s consistent structure and effects contributed to an intuitive user experience. Therefore, we would look to add more descriptions and images to make our website more appealing and informative but be cautious of adding too many effects.
  • This project helped show that brainstorming, collecting user feedback, and iterating over many different website designs helps create unique perspectives that lead to a more creative and effective end result.
  • Comparing our initial assumptions about workflow with our user testing feedback revealed any unintuitive or inconsistent interactions, and thus helped us improve our website.
